Dueling Duals
Capabilities vs. ACLs

 








 

XXX Epiphany: there are three levels of granularity: objects (fine), people in a company/org (medium), sovereign entities without a mutual authority scope (large). The large one needs COOPERATION WITHOUT VULNERABILITY. It is also the same as COMMERCE. The fine one is OO/caps/E/POLA. The middle one is currently dominated by ACLs...

  • XXX Change of perspective: think of principals as both humans and as programs (/processes/objects) rather than primarily as humans.
  • capabilities allow a principal to extend (a subset of) their privileges to another principal
  • capabilities enforce the Granovetter constraint

    in ACLs there are two kinds of access: access to resources and write-access to ACLs. In capabilities these are unified. Absolute difference, then: in capabilities the holder of a privilege can delegate that privilege to another principal without needing a separate "can delegate this privilege" privilege. Therefore: this delegated privilege can be further delegated by the xxxyouknowhwatimean recipient.

    Suppose you had a basic ACL system, where each access control list contains only a list of principals who have the privilege that the list governs. Now, if you add the feature that each principal in the list also has the privilege of appending other principals to the list, then your system now has delegation (but without revocation, as well as without the Granovetter constraint that we'll get to below...)

    • !!! different classes of resource and principal in ACLs means it isn't easy to express the "extend only if Granovetter" constraint/feature.
  • capabilities allow the proxy pattern -- an object which is both -- principal (holder, user of privileges) and a resource -- (access-controlled)
