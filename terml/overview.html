TermL Overview

TermL (pronounced "TermEl") stands for either Term Language or Terms for E Replace Markup Languages. TermL is E's framework for handling symbolic data, including XML, object serialization, programming language text, and more. Like the ISO seven layer protocol model, we propose a model of six layers of representations in which to organize the issues, shown in the table below, in which the representations range from more concrete ones at the bottom, to more abstract ones at the top.

The abstracting steps on the left are those going upwards, in which data represented at a lower layer is transformed into a representation at a higher layer. Those lower layer data representations that are acceptable as inputs to a given abstracting step are well formed with respect to that abstracting step. If a given abstracting step is able to produce all possible representations at a higher level, it is universal with respect to that higher layer representation, and its inputs are form language for writing such higher level representations in the lower level notation. If a given abstracting step maps only to a subset of the higher level representation, then exactly those higher level representations within this subset are valid with respect to that abstracting step, and we have a validating abstracting step. From an abstracting step one can derive a validity checker that checks that a given higher level representation is within this subset without reference to a low level representation that it would be extracted from.

The concretizing steps on the right are the inverses of the abstracting steps. The inverse of a universal abstracting step is a universal concretizing step, and is able to concretize any possible instance of the given higher level representation.

For most data representation standards that we deal with, we will try to say what subset of this model they correspond to, and point out ways they don't quite fit the model.
